Introduction

This webpage is a reimplementation of a program written by László Lovász
László Lovász is a Hungarian mathematician, whose research spans combinatorial optimization, algorithms, complexity, graph theory and random walks. He was awarded the 2021 Abel prize jointly with Avi Wigderson. For more, see his Wikipedia page or his homepage. . The original is a Windows program, and some of its functionality doesn't work on modern Windows. Lovász dedicated the program to the memory of W.T.Tutte. The page assumes basic knowledge of graph theory.

This program illustrates how one simple mathematical idea: replacing the edges of a graph by rubber bands, can be used to design a variety of graph algorithms:

  • drawing a planar graph in the plane, a method of Tutte (1963);
  • connectivity testing, an algorithm of Linial, Lovász and Wigderson (1988);
  • maximum cut , an approximation algorithm of Goemans and Williamson (1994);
  • squaring a rectangle, a construction of Brooks, Smith, Stone and Tutte (1940).
