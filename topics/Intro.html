Introduction

This webpage is a reimplementation of a program written by László Lovász László Lovász is a Hungarian mathematician, whose research spans combinatorial optimization, algorithms, complexity, graph theory and random walks. He was awarded the 2021 Abel prize jointly with Avi Wigderson. For more, see his Wikipedia page or his homepage. . The original is a Windows program, and some of its functionality doesn't work on modern Windows. Lovász dedicated the program to the memory of W.T.Tutte William Thomas Tutte (1917-2002): British-Canadian mathematician, who made fundamental contributions to graph theory. For more, see https://uwaterloo.ca/combinatorics-and-optimization/professor-william-t-tutte. . The page assumes basic knowledge of graph theory.

This program illustrates how one simple mathematical idea: replacing the edges of a graph by rubber bands, can be used to design a variety of graph algorithms:

  • drawing a planar graph A graph is planar if it can be drawn in the plane so that the edges are continuous curves that do not intersect each other. Such a drawing is called an embedding of the graph in the plane. in the plane, a method of Tutte (1963) W.T. Tutte: How to draw a graph, Proc. London Math. Soc. 13 (1963), 743-768. ;
  • connectivity A graph is connected if any two of its nodes can be reached from each other on a path.
    A graph is k-connected if it is connected and it stays connected if fewer than k of its nodes are deleted.
